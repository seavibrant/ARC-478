Experiment Log 714-1 - "Audio"

RESEARCHER: real_skydish

RANK: Entrant

RELEVANT SCP(s): SCP-714

AUTHORIZATION: N/A

SUPERVISION: N/A

COMBATIVE(s) Intrusivenez

TEST SUBJECT(s) deathshotplays8101

RATIONALE:

What effect does ambient noise and music have on subjects wearing SCP-714?

HYPOTHESIS:

Since SCP-714 has several mental effects on subjects wearing it, in particular slowed reactions and reduced mental capacity, the subject may be unable to describe certain aspects of the noise/music they are hearing.

PROCEDURE:

Once I had taken the needed audio tapes from the archive, I begun by requesting a Class-D and combative to transfer C over the radio, both of which were quickly on the spot. The CD was given the agreement paper and I asked for the combatant's ID. I then gave the CD a light briefing on what the test would inflict upon him, in this case no harm since SCP-714 does not have any harmful effects on the wearer. Once finished we moved into Sector-2B and headed towards the item storage. On the way, Adept Researcher MCJu3464 joined us and oversaught the test, he had said that he would on the radio but I had missed it. Once at the item storage, someone opened the storage container for SCP-714 and the CD took it, he was then asked to give it to the combative, he complied with this order.

The group then headed for the MPCC where the CD was further briefed on the contents of the test. He was told to wait with putting on the ring until I gave him the order, and to listen to the music and ambience provided by me. The CD was then told to enter the inner chamber and wait for my next instructions. But before that, MCJu asked everyone for their IDs, I was unaware that this had to be done in the chamber but I am now a bit wiser regarding that.

Once the CD had entered the chamber, he was told to listen to the "City Ambience" tape once before putting on the ring. Upon being asked if he could hear the tape, he answered "No" but a short moment later changed his answer to "Yes", mentioning that his volume was low. The combatant then asked me if he should send the ring down, to which I answered yes. The CD recieved the ring and was told to put it on, after confirming that he was wearing it, he was asked if he could hear any differences in the audio. To this he answered with "sounds a bit more upbeat". After this regard, I changed the tape to the one labeled "Goofy Song". The CD was then asked what he could tell regarding this audio, to which he answered with "no changes, sounds goofy.". The CD was then told to remove the ring, and to listen to the music again, this proved to have no effect as he said that taking the ring off provided no change in the audio. Lastly, the CD was told to put the ring back on and listen to the "Night Ambience" tape. He was then asked what he could tell about this audio, to which he answered with "makes me picture a forest, placid music, very calming.". Once I had removed the last audio tape, the CD was told to remove the ring, send it up the chute, and exit the chamber. The CD was asked if he had any final remarks about the test, to which he said no. Right as the test was concluded, a riot was initiated and the CD was swiftly terminated by the combatant, who then returned SCP-714 to the item storage.

CONCLUSION:

The experiment showed minimal changes in auditory perception due to wearing SCP-714. The test subject's ability to describe the auditory stimuli remained intact and consistent, with minor differences in the interpretation of sound reported while wearing the ring. The key observations I made during this test were, but not limited to:

  1. Subject's ability to hear and describe ambient noise and music was not significantly affected by SCP-714.
  2. Subject's perception of sound quality and mood was unaltered while wearing SCP-714, with minor changes in the interpretation of audio stimuli.
  3. Subject's mental capacity and reaction time were not significantly impacted by SCP-714 during the test.

When the ring was removed and the audio was replayed, no difference in perception was noted.

I can therefore conclude that while SCP-714 may have other effects on subjects wearing it, it does not in any notable way affect nor impair the subject's perception of auditory stimuli.
